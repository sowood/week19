TYPES OF TOOLS

There are many types of tools used in woodworking.
Previous Next

Hand Tools

Saws are used for cutting wood into different shapes, usually into panels and rails.

Saws can be classified into two main groups, crosscut and rip. These classifications are based upon the shape of their teeth and their intended purpose.

Crosscut saws are designed to cut across the wood grain and have knife-like teeth that cut the wood fibers as they traverse across the wood's surface

Rip saws are meant to cut with the grain and have chisle-like teeth that "rip" out a channel of wood fibers as they cut.

Chisels are used to remove material from a piece by cutting our gauging the material out. The cutting end of a chisel is beveled and sharpened while the handle end is designed to be struck with a mallet.
A plane is a tool used to flatten the surface of a plank or panel. It is composed of a flat body that rides ontop of the work surface and a cutting blade that is lowered through the body at an angle so that a minute amount of material is removed with each pass. A surface that is uneven will have high and low spots. As the plane cuts across the surface, it removes the high spots until the surface is eventually even.
